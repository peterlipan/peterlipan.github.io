Li Pan

Hi! I am a first-year PhD student at the University of Edinburgh. My research is generously funded by the European Research Council (ERC) and College of Medicine and Veterinary Medicine (CMVM), under the supervision of Professor Miguel Bernabeu. My primary focus is on developing artificial intelligence solutions for real-world clinical challenges, covering a wide range from AI applications in oncology to broader AI implementations in healthcare. I also work closely with Dr. Zhen Chen on medical image analysis.

My research interests revolve around quantitatively assessing human health conditions using multi-omics data, which encompasses genomics data, pathological imaging, and privacy-preserving sensing. I am committed to discovering digital biomarkers of diseases and uncovering the underlying mechanisms. I obtained my B.E. degree in Computer Science and Technology from Xiamen University, China. I earned my MSc degree with distinction (ranked 1/59) from the City University of Hong Kong.
